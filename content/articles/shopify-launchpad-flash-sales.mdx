Product drops and flash sales are high-stakes events. A misconfigured discount, a theme that doesn't load, or a price that fails to revert after a sale can cost thousands in margin or damage customer trust. Shopify Launchpad — available exclusively to Plus merchants — removes the manual risk by scheduling every moving part of a campaign in advance.

## What Launchpad Handles

- Scheduled theme publishing and reverting
- Price changes across selected products or collections
- Discount code activation and deactivation
- Script changes (e.g., buy-one-get-one activating at midnight)
- Sales channel visibility toggles
- Storefront password enable and disable

## Planning a Flash Sale Event

Begin by mapping every element of your campaign: which products are involved, what the discount percentage is, whether you need a sale-specific theme or banner, and exactly when the sale starts and ends. Launchpad lets you create an event with a start time and end time, then attach each action to either the start or the end of the event.

## Theme Scheduling for Campaign Pages

Many brands maintain a second theme variant with campaign-specific hero banners, countdown timers, and promotional messaging. Launchpad can publish this theme at your event start and revert to your standard theme automatically at the end — no manual publish required and no risk of forgetting to switch back.

### Best Practice: Theme Preparation Checklist

1. Duplicate your live theme before making campaign modifications
2. Test all campaign theme changes in a preview before scheduling
3. Ensure countdown timers use the correct timezone
4. Verify all promotional images are optimised for page speed
5. Test on mobile before scheduling the theme go-live

## Managing Product Drops

For limited-edition product drops, Launchpad can control product visibility — keeping items hidden until exactly the right moment. This is particularly valuable for streetwear, collectibles, and beauty launches where the anticipation element is part of the brand experience.

<Callout>
Always schedule a Launchpad test event 24 hours before a major campaign to verify all actions execute correctly in your live environment.
</Callout>

## Post-Event Reversion

The reversion phase is as important as the launch. Configure Launchpad to restore original prices, deactivate discount codes, and republish your standard theme at the event end time. This prevents the common (and costly) mistake of leaving sale prices active for days after a campaign has ended.

## Limitations to Be Aware Of

Launchpad does not currently support scheduling metafield changes, complex inventory operations, or changes to third-party app configurations. For anything outside its scope, pair Launchpad with Shopify Flow to cover additional automation requirements.

> A well-configured Launchpad event means your team can focus on customer experience on launch day, rather than scrambling to change prices and swap themes at midnight.
